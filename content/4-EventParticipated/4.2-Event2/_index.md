# Report on "Career, DevOps & Cloud Meetup"

### Event Objectives

- Sharing practical experiences from engineers currently working in businesses.

- Introducing career development paths in the Cloud, DevOps, and Data fields.

- Helping students better understand the work culture at multinational corporations.

- Sharing some practical system architectures deployed on AWS.

### Speaker List

- Kien & Tho – A Scalable URL Shortening Service on AWS.

- Danh Hoang Hieu Nghi – From First Cloud AI Journey to AWS Partner.

- Trong H. Truong – What does a DevOps Engineer really do?

- Dat Pham & Cuong Nguyen – Data Analytics Engineers and Multinational Corporate Culture.

### Highlights

#### Designing a URL Shortening Service on AWS

Speakers will introduce how to build a scalable URL shortening service on AWS. Through cloud architecture, the system is designed to ensure performance, scalability, and availability. The presentation helped me understand that even a seemingly simple problem requires consideration of architecture, traffic, scalability, and cost optimization.

#### The Journey from FCAJ to AWS Partner

The speaker shared their journey from the First Cloud AI Journey program to becoming an AWS Community Builder and working at an AWS Partner. What impressed me most was the message, "Getting the job is just a beginning." Getting a job is just the start; what's more important is continuous learning, community building, knowledge sharing, and long-term skill development.

#### What does a DevOps Engineer really do?

The presentation helped me understand that DevOps is not just Docker, Kubernetes, or CI/CD. DevOps is a mindset that connects Development and Operations to make software development, deployment, and operation more efficient. The speaker emphasized the importance of Linux, networking, Git, containers, infrastructure as code, logging, monitoring, and automation capabilities.

A key lesson learned was that one shouldn't just copy commands but understand the core of the problem. Always asking "Why?" before "How?" leads to more effective problem-solving. Beyond technical skills, communication and teamwork are also crucial elements of a DevOps Engineer.

#### Data Analytics and Corporate Culture

The speakers shared the practical work of a Data Analytics Engineer in a business. Beyond building dashboards, data analysts must analyze root causes, uncover insights, and propose solutions to support business operations.

In addition to professional skills, the speaker introduced important competencies such as critical thinking, communication, storytelling with data, and problem-solving. The career development model from Follower, Learner, Problem Solver to System Thinker gave me a clearer understanding of the long-term development process.

The presentation on multinational corporate culture was also very impressive. Values ​​such as No-Blame Post-Mortem, respect for diversity, continuous learning, and collaboration between departments contribute to a professional work environment.

### What I Learned

#### Professional Knowledge

- Gained a deeper understanding of scalable cloud system design.

- Gained a more realistic perspective on the work of a DevOps Engineer and Data Analytics Engineer.

- Learned essential foundational skills such as Linux, Networking, Git, Containers, and Cloud.

- Understood the role of Data Analytics in supporting decision-making.

#### Career Orientation

- Proactively built personal projects and a portfolio.

- Participated in the technology community to learn and expand my network.

- Continuously updated my knowledge instead of just studying for certifications.

### Application to Work

- Continue learning AWS and DevOps following a structured roadmap.

- Practice building projects for deployment on the Cloud.

- Develop data analysis and presentation skills.

- Apply systems thinking and automation to personal projects.

- Better prepare for internships and interviews at companies.

### Experience at the Event

The meeting provided many practical perspectives from professionals working in businesses, rather than just focusing on theoretical knowledge. I was particularly impressed with the sharing about career development journeys, how to build a technical foundation, and a professional work mindset.

Through the presentations, I realized that a solid foundation is always more important than chasing after too many new technologies. Understanding the nature of the system, knowing how to communicate, proactively learning, and continuously practicing will create a long-term advantage in my career.

Besides technical knowledge, the Meetup also helped me better understand the work culture at multinational corporations, the recruitment process, and the skills that businesses truly expect from recent graduates.
